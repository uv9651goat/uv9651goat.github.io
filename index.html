Technical Depth

Engineering Research

⬡

Suspension Architecture Study

Comparative analysis of rover suspension systems for off-road terrain. Evaluating stability, obstacle clearance, and load distribution across architectures.

  • Rocker-Bogie: best stability, 6-wheel independence
  • Dual Bogie: reduced complexity, lower mass
  • Triple Bogie: maximum terrain adaptability
  • ANSYS load distribution validation
  • Obstacle-climb ratio analysis (wheel dia. vs. step height)
⚙

Cycloidal Gearbox Design

High-reduction cycloidal drive design for robotic joint actuation. Compact form factor, low backlash, and high shock-load tolerance.

  • Reduction ratio: 27:1 single stage
  • Cycloidal disc profile generation via parametric CAD
  • FEA stress analysis on disc & pin rollers
  • Efficiency: ~92% at rated torque
  • Backlash: <0.1° under load
∿

Manipulator Kinematics

Full kinematic analysis of the 5-DoF robotic arm using DH parameters. MATLAB-based workspace visualization and trajectory planning.

  • Forward kinematics via DH parameter table
  • Inverse kinematics — geometric & analytical method
  • Jacobian-based velocity kinematics
  • Reachable workspace volume computation
  • Singularity analysis & avoidance strategy
◈

Wheel-Terrain Interaction

Terramechanics study for rover wheel design on simulated Martian regolith. Traction force, sinkage, and slip ratio analysis.

  • Bekker-Wong soil model implementation
  • Grouser geometry optimization for loose sand
  • Traction vs. sinkage trade-off analysis
  • Wheel torque requirement calculations
△

FEA Structural Analysis

Finite element analysis of critical rover chassis components under static and dynamic loading conditions using ANSYS Mechanical.

  • Static structural analysis: 3g shock load
  • Von Mises stress contours & safety factor
  • Modal analysis — natural frequency mapping
  • Material: Al 6061-T6 & PETG composites
⊕

Impeller & Airflow CFD

Computational fluid dynamics simulation of the cleaning robot impeller for optimal suction pressure and flow rate.

  • Blade angle optimization: 35° backward curved
  • Pressure-flow (PQ) curve generation
  • BLDC motor speed-torque matching
  • Noise reduction via blade count study
About Me

The Engineer Behind the Machines

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at IIITDM Kancheepuram (CGPA 8.7), currently serving as Mechanical Lead at MaRS Research Station — leading a team of 20+ engineers in robotics system development. My work spans CAD modeling, FEA, and motion analysis, with hands-on experience building autonomous manipulators, rovers, and cleaning robots.

I've engineered triple-bogie, rocker-bogie, and dual-bogie suspension architectures, designed cycloidal gearboxes handling 200+ kg·cm torque, and developed 5–6 DoF manipulators with MATLAB-based kinematic simulations — all the way from design to field-tested competition hardware.

I believe robotics systems should be designed through the integration of mechanical robustness, efficient actuation, and intelligent control. My work focuses on building reliable mechanical architectures that enable robots to operate in challenging environments.
